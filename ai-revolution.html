AI Revolution: From Perceptron to Transformer

The journey began with the perceptron in the 1950s, a simple model mimicking a biological neuron. It could make linear decisions but failed at solving XOR problems.

Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) improved this by stacking layers, enabling non-linear decision boundaries. RNNs introduced memory for sequences. Transformers replaced recurrence with attention, enabling models like GPT.
